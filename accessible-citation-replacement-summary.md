# Accessible Citation Replacement Summary

## Overview
Successfully replaced 17 inaccessible academic book citations with publicly accessible alternatives while maintaining academic quality and cultural authenticity.

## Problem Identified
- Academic publisher URLs (McGraw-Hill, Pearson, Oxford, Cambridge, etc.) often require institutional access
- Incomplete URLs pointing to publisher homepages rather than specific resources
- Users unable to access referenced materials despite academic quality

## Solution Implemented
Replaced inaccessible citations with:
- **Educational Resources**: Open-access music theory sites (musictheory.net, Open Music Theory)
- **Cultural Resources**: Specialized cultural websites for specific traditions
- **Ethnomusicological Resources**: Academic institutions' open collections (Smithsonian Folkways)
- **Specialized Databases**: Domain-specific sites like maqamworld.com for Middle Eastern music
